1. Overview and Accountability

This Privacy Policy explains how the Dunsin Sunday Fakorede Ward 5 Grantham Campaign ("the Campaign", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects personal information gathered through this website and campaign activities.

We are committed to compliance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), Canada's federal private sector privacy law, and all applicable provincial privacy legislation in Ontario.

The Campaign is responsible for all personal information under its control. Questions or concerns about this policy may be directed to the contact details in Section 11.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information only when you voluntarily provide it through one of the following forms on this website:

Volunteer Form — name, email address, phone number, neighbourhood, volunteer preferences, availability, and any message you choose to include.

Lawn Sign Request Form — name, service address, phone number, and email address.

Donation Record Form — full legal name, email address, phone number, home address, donation amount, payment method, and date. Home address is required by the Municipal Elections Act, 1996 for election finance reporting.

Contact Form — name, email address, subject, and your message.

We also collect anonymous, aggregated website usage data through Google Analytics (see Section 9).

3. Why We Collect It

Personal information is collected for the following purposes:

  • Coordinating volunteer activities and canvassing operations
  • Delivering requested lawn signs to your address
  • Issuing donation receipts and complying with Ontario election finance reporting requirements under the Municipal Elections Act, 1996
  • Responding to general inquiries, media requests, and event invitations
  • Sending campaign updates and communications to supporters who have signed up to receive them
  • Understanding how visitors use this website in order to improve its content (analytics only)

We will identify the purpose for collecting personal information before or at the time of collection.

4. Consent

By submitting any form on this website, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information as described in this Privacy Policy and for the purpose identified at the time of collection.

You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us using the details in Section 11. Note that withdrawing consent may limit our ability to provide certain services (for example, coordinating a volunteer role or delivering a lawn sign).

Consent for donation record information cannot be fully withdrawn where retention is required by law under the Municipal Elections Act, 1996.

5. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used only for the purposes identified in Section 3. We do not:

  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party
  • Use your information for commercial marketing unrelated to the campaign
  • Share your information with other political candidates or parties
  • Use your information for purposes other than those stated at the time of collection without obtaining new consent

6. Sharing and Disclosure

Your personal information may be shared in limited circumstances:

  • Campaign team members — campaign staff and volunteers involved in coordinating the activity you signed up for
  • Netlify Inc. — our website hosting and form processing provider. Form submissions are processed through Netlify Forms and stored on Netlify's servers. Netlify's privacy policy is available at netlify.com
  • Election authorities — donation information is disclosed to the City of St. Catharines as required by the Municipal Elections Act, 1996 for public financial reporting after the election
  • Legal requirement — if required by law, regulation, or lawful court order

We do not share your information in any other circumstances without your explicit consent.

7. Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law.

  • Volunteer and contact information — retained for the duration of the 2026 campaign period and up to six months after the election, then securely deleted unless you request continued communications
  • Donation records — retained for a minimum of four years after the election as required under the Municipal Elections Act, 1996
  • Analytics data — Google Analytics retains aggregated usage data per Google's standard retention settings. This data contains no personally identifiable information

8. Security and Safeguards

We implement reasonable and appropriate security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification.

  • This website is served over HTTPS with TLS encryption
  • Form submissions are transmitted securely via Netlify's encrypted pipeline
  • Access to form submission data is restricted to authorised campaign team members
  • We do not store credit card or banking information — no payment processing occurs on this website

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we take all reasonable precautions to protect your information.

9. Cookies and Analytics

This website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to understand how visitors use the site and to improve its content.

Google Analytics 4 — We use Google Analytics to collect anonymous, aggregated information about website visits, including pages viewed, time on site, and general geographic region. Google Analytics does not collect personally identifiable information. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Consent cookie — When you accept our cookie notice, a small cookie named cc is stored in your browser's local storage to remember your preference and prevent the banner from reappearing.

Managing cookies — You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some functionality of this website. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites

10. Your Rights

Under PIPEDA, you have the right to:

  • Access — request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction — request that inaccurate or incomplete information be corrected
  • Withdrawal of consent — withdraw consent to the collection, use, or disclosure of your information (subject to legal and contractual restrictions)
  • Complaint — file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 11. We will respond to access requests within 30 days.

You also have the right to file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada: priv.gc.ca · 1-800-282-1376.

12. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or website features. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of this website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
